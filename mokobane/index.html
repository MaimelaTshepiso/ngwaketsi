Frequently Asked Questions

What types of lifting equipment do you inspect and certify?

We inspect and certify a wide range of lifting equipment including cranes, hoists, winches, chain blocks, lever hoists, slings, shackles, and other lifting accessories. Our services cover equipment used in mining, construction, industrial, and power plant applications.

How often should lifting equipment be inspected?

The frequency of inspections depends on the type of equipment and its usage. Generally, lifting equipment should undergo:

  • Pre-use checks: Daily
  • Functional tests: Weekly to monthly
  • Thorough examinations: Every 6-12 months as per OSHA regulations

We can help you establish an appropriate inspection schedule based on your specific equipment and usage patterns.

What certifications do your inspectors hold?

Our inspectors are certified professionals with qualifications including:

  • Registered Lifting Machinery Inspector (LMI)
  • OSHA compliant certification
  • Specialized training in specific equipment types
  • Years of field experience in mining and industrial environments
What is included in a typical inspection service?

Our comprehensive inspection service includes:

  • Visual examination of all components
  • Functional testing of operational controls
  • Measurement of wear and tear on critical parts
  • Load testing where required
  • Documentation of findings
  • Certification report with pass/fail status
  • Recommendations for repairs or maintenance
Do you provide emergency repair services?

Yes, we offer emergency repair services for critical lifting equipment failures. Our team can typically respond within 24-48 hours for emergency situations, depending on your location. We understand that equipment downtime can be costly, especially in mining operations, so we prioritize urgent repair requests.
